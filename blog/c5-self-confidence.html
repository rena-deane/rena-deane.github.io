Self-Confidence

Self-awareness is mindfulness and it’s explained through using a traditional analogy. Our minds are the fluttering flag. If the pole practiced self awareness, the pole maintains self-reflectiveness even in the midst of turbulent emotions - the fluttering flag. The mindful pole pays attention to the present moment without judgement. Both states are not distracted by the fluttering flag.

I practice Journalling often and it’s a liberating experience. I can be in a mindful state throughout the time I journal and this is where I notice I don’t judge any feelings, thoughts, ideas that I have. I don’t give myself time to think I write continuously. You always feel that little bit lighter when you’ve completed a good journaling session.

My favourite part of this chapter was reading, “Thoughts and emotions are like the clouds - some beautiful, some dark - while our core being is the sky. Clouds are not the sky; they are phenomena in the sky that come and go. Similarly, thoughts and emotions are not who we are; they are simply phenomena in mind and body that come and go.” My brain thinks all the time and I have alot of nice and dark thoughts. To read this metaphor takes the power away from the thoughts and emotions I have. Even though I think of something or feel a certain way, it doesn’t define me, it’s not who I am. It’s a beautiful way of explaining it.
