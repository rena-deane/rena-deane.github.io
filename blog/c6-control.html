Triggers

I found this chapter really interesting. STOP, BREATH, NOTICE, REFLECT, RESPOND. The SiBerian North RailRoad to help recognise and handle trigger and emotions that arise from them.

This is a timely lesson now that we're getting towards the bootcamp. I've had a few scenarios where I've reacted straight away to things that annoy me. So now I can follow this technique.

  • Stop, for the sacred pause.
  • Take a deep breath.
  • Notice how my body is reacting and feeling, and the tension and temperature changes.
  • Reflect on where the emotion is coming from. Is it historical? Does it involve another person? Is there self-perceived inadequacy?
  • How do we respond to the situation?

Assesing triggers and why we have triggers is a form of healing. Knowing the root cause of our reaction gives us insight in to how past events affect us if we don't let go and how we're wired.
