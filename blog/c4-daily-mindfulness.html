Daily Mindfulness

Mindfulness Conversation

I learnt that there are three key components to a mindful conversation.

  1. Mindful listening - giving the the person you are talking to your full attention. Do not interrupt them but acknowledge that you hear what they are saying.
  2. Looping - short for “closing the loop of communication”. When your are listening to someone you loop back by telling that person what you heard them say. You can check to see if anything was misinterpreted or misunderstood. This practice can be hugely beneficial in a collaborative environment.
  3. Dipping - or checking in with ourselves. This deals with those times our minds wander when we listen and when we’re speaking. When we’re distracted or we react internally to what has been said we should notice and acknowledge them. SIY puts it simply as “self-directed mindfulness during listening”.

As I was reading this chapters I recognised everything that was mentioned and thought back to past conversations. I was aware when I interrupted someone or when my mind was wandering. I would do my best to pull my attention back to the person I was talking to. I haven’t practiced mindful conversations knowing what it was, but now that I know it’s a thing it lets me know that I’m not too far off. It’s good to be aware of this especially because we’ll be doing a lot of collaborating during bootcamp. I look forward to applying these techniques during my next conversations.

To sustain my practice I will take one mindful breath a day. Surely I can manage that. I’ll have to set a reminder to do it.

Somemore Mindulness Stuff

Adding to everything I've stated above I would like to practise is walking meditation. Remembering to appreciate all the different things we do on a day to day basis. Things we often take for granted.
